# Project Success & The Project Manager

> "A project delivered on time and within budget is not necessarily a successful project."

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this lesson, you should be able to:

- Define project success.
- Explain the role of the Project Manager.
- Describe the responsibilities of the Project Sponsor.
- Distinguish project success from project management success.
- Understand leadership skills required for project managers.

---

# What is Project Success?

People often assume that a successful project simply means finishing on time and within budget.

While these are important measures, they do not tell the whole story.

Imagine delivering a product exactly on schedule, but the customer never uses it.

Would you call that a success?

Probably not.

True project success means creating value for the organization and satisfying the project's stakeholders.

---

# The Iron Triangle

Traditionally, project success has been measured using three constraints:

- Scope
- Time
- Cost

These are often called the **Iron Triangle**.

Changing one of these usually affects the other two.

For example:

Increasing project scope often increases both cost and duration.

---

# Beyond the Iron Triangle

Modern project management considers additional success factors such as:

- Customer satisfaction
- Product quality
- Business value
- Risk management
- Team satisfaction
- Sustainability
- Long-term organizational benefits

Projects are increasingly evaluated based on whether they deliver strategic value.

---

# Example

A company develops a new customer portal.

The project:

- Finished one month early.
- Stayed under budget.
- Met every technical requirement.

However, customers disliked the interface and continued using the old system.

Although the project met the Iron Triangle, it failed to deliver business value.

---

# The Project Manager

The Project Manager is responsible for leading the project from planning to completion.

Typical responsibilities include:

- Planning work
- Leading the team
- Managing risks
- Monitoring progress
- Controlling costs
- Managing communication
- Reporting status
- Solving problems

The Project Manager coordinates people, resources and information.

---

# Leadership vs Management

Project managers do much more than scheduling.

They must also lead people.

Management focuses on:

- Planning
- Organizing
- Controlling

Leadership focuses on:

- Inspiring
- Motivating
- Building trust
- Handling conflict

The best project managers combine both.

---

# Essential Skills

Successful project managers often demonstrate:

## Communication

Clear communication prevents misunderstandings.

---

## Decision-making

Projects require daily decisions, often with incomplete information.

---

## Problem-solving

Unexpected issues appear in almost every project.

---

## Negotiation

Resources are limited.

Project managers frequently negotiate priorities with stakeholders.

---

## Time Management

Balancing deadlines and resources is essential.

---

## Emotional Intelligence

Understanding people's motivations often determines project success more than technical expertise.

---

# The Project Sponsor

The Project Sponsor provides:

- Funding
- Strategic direction
- Organizational support
- Major approvals

The Sponsor owns the business case.

The Project Manager delivers the project.

These roles complement each other.

---

# Common Reasons Projects Fail

Projects rarely fail because of only one issue.

Common causes include:

- Poor communication
- Weak leadership
- Unclear objectives
- Lack of stakeholder engagement
- Unrealistic schedules
- Poor risk management
- Changing requirements
- Lack of executive support

Many of these problems can be reduced through effective project management.

---

# Case Study

A logistics company implements a new warehouse management system.

The Project Manager delivers the system on schedule.

However:

Employees receive almost no training.

Many continue using spreadsheets.

Warehouse productivity decreases.

The project technically succeeded.

The business outcome failed.

This illustrates the difference between project delivery and project success.

---

# Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| Project Success | Achieving intended business outcomes and stakeholder value. |
| Iron Triangle | Scope, Time and Cost. |
| Project Manager | Person responsible for leading the project. |
| Project Sponsor | Senior executive responsible for business value. |
| Leadership | Influencing and motivating people toward project goals. |

---

# Summary

Successful projects deliver more than schedules and budgets.

Great project managers:

- communicate effectively
- solve problems
- lead people
- manage risks
- create business value

Always remember:

> Delivering the project is not the same as delivering success.
