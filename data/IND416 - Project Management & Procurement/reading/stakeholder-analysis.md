# Stakeholder Analysis

> "Projects do not fail only because of technical problems. They often fail because people are forgotten."

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this lesson, you should be able to:

- Define what a stakeholder is.
- Identify internal and external stakeholders.
- Perform a stakeholder analysis.
- Understand the Power-Interest Matrix.
- Develop strategies for managing stakeholders.
- Explain why stakeholder management is critical to project success.

---

# What is a Stakeholder?

A stakeholder is any individual, group or organization that can influence a project or be affected by it.

Some stakeholders make decisions.

Others provide resources.

Some simply experience the consequences of the project.

Ignoring an important stakeholder is one of the most common reasons projects fail.

- Local residents

- IT Department

---

## External

People outside the organization.

Examples:

- Customers
- Suppliers
- Government
- Communities
- Shareholders
- Media

# Why Stakeholder Analysis Matters

Every stakeholder has:

- Different goals
- Different priorities
- Different levels of influence

A supplier may want higher prices.

The finance department wants lower costs.

Customers want faster delivery.

The project manager must balance all of these interests.

---

# The Power-Interest Matrix

One of the most common stakeholder tools is the Power-Interest Matrix.

It divides stakeholders into four groups.

## High Power + High Interest

Manage Closely

Examples:

- Sponsor
- CEO
- Major customer

These stakeholders require regular communication.

---

## High Power + Low Interest

Keep Satisfied

Examples:

- Board of Directors
- Government regulators

They should receive updates but not unnecessary details.

---

## Low Power + High Interest

Keep Informed

Examples:

- Employees
- End users

They want information but usually cannot influence major decisions.

---

## Low Power + Low Interest

Monitor

These stakeholders require minimal attention.

Wants lower purchasing costs.

---

# Common Mistakes

## Forgetting stakeholders

Sometimes project teams only communicate with management.

Later they discover that employees reject the new system.

---

## Assuming everyone supports the project

Some stakeholders benefit.

Others may lose influence, responsibilities or income.

Expect resistance.

---

## Poor communication

Stakeholders cannot support a project they do not understand.

Communication should be planned from the beginning.

---

# Stakeholder Register

Projects often maintain a Stakeholder Register containing:

- Name
- Organization
- Role
- Interest
- Influence
- Communication frequency
- Responsible team member

The register is updated throughout the project.

# Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| Stakeholder | Anyone affected by or able to influence the project. |
| Stakeholder Analysis | Process of identifying and assessing stakeholders. |
| Power | Ability to influence project decisions. |
| Interest | Degree to which the stakeholder cares about the project. |
| Stakeholder Register | Document containing stakeholder information. |

---

# Summary

Successful projects manage people as carefully as they manage budgets and schedules.

Remember:

- Identify stakeholders early.
- Understand their interests.
- Prioritize them.
- Communicate appropriately.
- Reassess throughout the project.

Stakeholder management is a continuous process—not a one-time exercise.
